Tom Kha Gai

What is Tom Kha?

Tom Kha originated in Thailand, and is one of the countries most famous dishes. This flavoursome soup traditionally consists of: Bird's Eye Chilli's, Kaffir Lime Leaves, Mushrooms, Coconut Milk, Galangal, Lemongrass, and Chicken. These ingredients combine to create a wonderfully aromatic dish which is bursting with flavour, as well as being highly nutritious.


Watch the video below for the perfect example of how to make a Tom Kha Gai!


Below, I will detail the ingredients and method for making my take on Tom Kha Gai. I removed the Coconut Milk to give it a thinner, less creamy consistency but this is entirely based on preference. Most ingredients will be available at your local supermarket, however, some may be difficult to find.

Ingredients:

  • Large Tomatoes - 4 (Diced)
  • Chicken - 2 Breasts (Diced, thick chunks)
  • Vegetable Stock - 1.25 Litres
  • Soy Sauce - 3 Tablespoons
  • Honey - 1 Tablespoon
  • Coriander - For Garnish
  • Lime Juice - 1 Lime
  • Coconut Oil - 1 Tablespoon
  • Galangal - 1 Tablespoon (Minced)
  • Garlic - 5 Cloves (Minced)
  • Lemongrass - 3 Stalks
  • Bird's Eye Chilli's - 5 Chilli's
  • Kaffir Lime Leaves - 3 Leaves
  • Closed Cup Mushrooms - 5 (Cut into 6)
  • Salt - To taste

Method:

  1. Grind up the Chilli, Galangal, and Garlic using a pestle & mortar until it forms a paste. Set this to one side. Chop the Lemongrass stalks into threes, then, using the back of a knife, lightly crush the pieces to release the oils.
  2. Heat up the Coconut Oil on medium heat until the oil begins to shimmer. When the oil has reached this stage, add the Lemongrass.
  3. Once the Lemongrass has become fragrant (around 3 minutes), add the chilli paste you made earlier and cook out for another 3 minutes making sure to stir occasionally.
  4. Next, add the chicken and fry off for 2 minutes or until the sides are sealed.
  5. Then, add the vegetable stock, lime leaves, soy sauce, and tomatoes. Stir to combine and bring to a boil. Once boiling, cover with a lid and lower the heat. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  6. After this, add the mushrooms and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Then, add the honey and lime juice, and add salt to taste.
  8. Finally, add Coriander leaves as a garnish and serve.

The Finished Dish

Below, is a picture of my finished Tom Kha. As you can see, my version is quite a clear soup. Not using coconut milk allowed for this thinner consistency which I chose due simply to preference. The lime leaves especially add a huge flavour and fragrance to this dish, making it a very pleasant experience to cook as well as to eat.
